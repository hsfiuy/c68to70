Campus Life

School-Church

Was established in 1978

A church school is a private educational institution that is affiliated with a religious organization. These schools are often founded and run by religious groups, and they typically offer a curriculum that is infused with religious teachings and values. Church schools can be found at all levels of education, from preschool to university. They are often known for their strong sense of community and their emphasis on character development. Many church schools also offer extracurricular activities that are designed to help students grow both academically and spiritually.

School-Library

Was established in 1978

A school library is a place where students can access a wide range of books and other reading materials. It is an essential part of any educational institution, as it provides students with the resources they need to learn and grow. A school library typically contains books on a variety of subjects, including history, science, literature, and more. Students can use these books to supplement their classroom learning, conduct research for projects and assignments, or simply read for pleasure.

School-Auditorium

Was established in 1978

School auditoriums are an essential part of any school's infrastructure. They are designed to accommodate large gatherings such as assemblies, performances, and other events. The auditoriums are equipped with modern sound systems, lighting, and projectors to ensure that the audience can see and hear everything clearly. They also provide a platform for students to showcase their talents and creativity. Inaugurations of school auditoriums are often celebrated with great enthusiasm and are attended by school staff, students, and parents alike
